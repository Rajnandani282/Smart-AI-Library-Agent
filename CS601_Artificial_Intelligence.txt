COURSE: CS601 - Artificial Intelligence
DEPARTMENT: Computer Science
SEMESTER: 6th Semester
CREDITS: 4

COURSE DESCRIPTION:
A comprehensive introduction to AI covering search, knowledge representation, reasoning under uncertainty, and modern machine learning methods.

UNIT 1: Introduction to AI
- History and philosophy of AI
- Rational agents and environments
- State of the art applications
Recommended Books: Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach (Russell & Norvig)

UNIT 2: Search
- Uninformed search: BFS, DFS, UCS
- Informed search: A*, heuristics, admissibility
- Local search: hill climbing, simulated annealing, genetic algorithms
Recommended Books: Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach (Russell & Norvig)

UNIT 3: Knowledge Representation and Reasoning
- Propositional and first-order logic
- Inference procedures, resolution
- Knowledge graphs and ontologies
Recommended Books: Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach (Russell & Norvig)

UNIT 4: Probabilistic Reasoning
- Bayesian networks
- Hidden Markov Models
- Monte Carlo methods
Recommended Books: Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach (Russell & Norvig), Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning (Bishop)

UNIT 5: Learning
- Supervised and unsupervised learning review
- Reinforcement learning basics
- Deep learning for AI applications
Recommended Books: Deep Learning (Goodfellow), Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach (Russell & Norvig)

UNIT 6: NLP and Computer Vision
- Text classification, information extraction
- Object detection, image segmentation
Recommended Books: Natural Language Processing with Python (Bird), Computer Vision (Szeliski)

ASSESSMENT: Assignments (30%), Mid-term (30%), End-term (40%)
